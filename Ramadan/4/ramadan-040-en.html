The Last Ten Days: How to Reorganize Work Without Extra Pressure
Hello,

As the last ten days of Ramadan begin, the atmosphere changes.
Focus shifts
Energy patterns fluctuate
Spiritual commitments increase

In Saudi Arabia, these days have a special impact on the work environment.

The question: do we continue at the same pace or redesign the week smartly?
🟢
Work Environment Reality
During the last ten days:

Many employees change their sleep patterns
Some dedicate time to spiritual retreats
Evening attendance increases
Spiritual obligations rise

Continuing at the same work intensity often slows productivity.
The problem is not commitment, but ignoring the natural rhythm.
💡
Key Insight
The last ten days don’t need less productivity…
They need a different type of productivity.

Instead of launching new initiatives or long strategic meetings:
Close files • Organize tasks • Quiet review • Administrative cleanup

This respects the spirit of the month and doesn’t harm work.
🧠
Steps to Design a Smart Week
1- “Closing Week”:
Finish open tasks • No new projects • No major launches

2- Reduce meetings by half:
Long meetings consume more energy than they produce.

3- Clear message to the team:
“These days we focus on finishing open tasks; the rest after Eid.”
