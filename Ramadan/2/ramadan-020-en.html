Remote Work in Ramadan…
Real Support or Just Another Announcement?
Hello,

Every Ramadan, we see announcements from companies promoting:
Flexible hours
Reduced workdays
Or remote work

It's truly a beautiful idea.
But the more important question is:

Does flexibility reduce the workload…
Or just shift it to a different time?

The Bottom Line
Remote work during Ramadan succeeds only if two conditions are met:

1- Clear expectations
If work hours are shorter,
are goals also scaled down?

2- A culture that doesn't rely on immediate responses
Flexibility doesn't mean
being available all day on WhatsApp.
🧠
Where Things Usually Go Wrong
Moving meetings to night instead of reducing them
Compressing tasks into fewer days
Assuming "home = rest"

But many people during Ramadan:
Sleep less
Have more social commitments
Experience fluctuating energy levels

So flexibility without workload reduction
Creates silent pressure.
✅ How to Create a Smart Ramadan Policy?

1- Reduce priorities, don't just relocate them
Choose 2–3 main focus areas for the month. The rest can wait.

2- Make "no immediate responses" an announced culture
"Responding within 24 hours is sufficient during Ramadan"

3- Don't make nighttime an extension of work hours
Nights in Ramadan have a different meaning.
If they become all about meetings… the purpose is lost.
