Leadership Newsletter – The Middle Manager
The Middle Manager: Closer to Pressure… Farther from Decision
Hello,

In every growing company, a sensitive role emerges — often underestimated: the middle manager.
This person hears from senior leadership, supports the team, and is accountable for results… without being the final decision‑maker.

Michael Lopp, in his famous article “The Middle Manager”, described middle managers as the “shock absorbers” of organizations.
Harvard Business Review also highlighted this role as one of the most exhausting professional positions, because the person lives between two forces: leadership demands and team needs, with limited authority and often unclear boundaries.
⚖️
The Reality a Middle Manager Lives
High responsibility — limited authority
Unwritten psychological load — conflicting expectations

The middle manager is not a weak link, but often the role that absorbs the most pressure.
That’s why burnout starts quietly here: hesitation, internal silence, overreaction to small issues, and a constant feeling that “I am the problem.”
📊
Why This Role Is So Draining
HBR: Middle management is one of the most exhausting roles because it sits between two forces.
Michael Lopp: “Shock absorbers” – they receive pressure from above and prevent it from fully hitting the team.

The problem is not the person.
The problem is the position.
High accountability without enough authority, and mismatched expectations.
🛡️
Steps to Ease the Pressure
1. Name the pressure
Instead of “I can’t handle this,” say: I am under pressure from two directions with different expectations.

2. Ask for role clarity, not role expansion
The most important question for your leadership: What exactly is expected of me? And what is NOT part of my role?

3. Don’t become a permanent shield
Absorbing everything without release is a fast track to burnout.
The middle manager is not a weak link. They are the operational and emotional backbone of many teams.

Any organization that wants to grow consciously must acknowledge this burden… and build tools to support it.
