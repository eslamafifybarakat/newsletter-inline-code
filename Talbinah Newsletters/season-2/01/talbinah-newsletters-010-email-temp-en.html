Insight

The pressure experienced by the middle manager
is not due to weak personality
or lack of skill.

Its cause is their position.

They are in an area of:
High expectations
Limited authority
Unwritten psychological responsibility

This type of pressure is called in organizational psychology:
Role Strain
Stress resulting from role conflict and lack of clear boundaries.

üß† Why does burnout start here?

According to a study published in Harvard Business Review about middle managers:
Middle managers suffer from higher stress levels than employees
and sometimes higher than senior leadership

The reason?
Because they:
Are responsible for people
But do not own strategic decisions

Practical Application (Realistic)

1) Name the pressure by its name
Instead of: "I can't handle it"
Say: "My current role has conflicting expectations"
Naming reduces the feeling of failure.

2) Ask for role clarity, not role expansion
Ask your management:
"What exactly is expected of me at this stage? And what is outside my scope?"
This question protects you psychologically.

3) Don't try to be a permanent shield
Absorbing shocks without release leads to burnout.
The role is not to prevent all pain,
The role is to transmit it in a healthy way.
