Fasting and Leadership: How Your Nerves Change… Without You Noticing?
Hello,

During Ramadan, something changes in us…
Not just in meal timings,
But in our entire nervous rhythm.

Many leaders notice that they:
React faster • Are less patient • Or become more sensitive to small details

And they often explain it as: “Work pressure.”

But sometimes… It’s simply a natural physiological change.
🟢
What Does Research Say?
Multiple scientific reviews indicate that:

Changes in sleep patterns • Reduced sleep hours • Or interrupted sleep

Have a direct effect on:
Emotional regulation • Reaction speed • Decision-making quality

For example, a review in Sleep Medicine Reviews shows that sleep loss affects emotional regulation, making a person more prone to quick irritability.
Sleep loss and emotional regulation – Sleep Medicine Reviews

Specifically in Ramadan, studies published in medical journals indicate that:
Sleep patterns shift • Some people sleep less • Focus fluctuates throughout the day • And this is normal.
💡
Summary
The problem isn’t fasting.
The problem is ignoring the effects of sleep and energy changes, then blaming yourself or the team for natural reactions.

Leaders in Ramadan need:

Higher self-awareness • Greater forgiveness • Postponing sensitive decisions when necessary

Not extra strictness.
🧠
How to Lead Consciously During Ramadan?
1- Don’t make sensitive decisions while exhausted • If the matter is big, postpone it to hours of clarity.

2- Declare the month’s rhythm • Tell your team: “We reduce sharp debates this month and focus on clarity.” This gives everyone psychological space.

3- Monitor your sleep before your mood • Sometimes the solution isn’t “anger management” but managing your sleep.
