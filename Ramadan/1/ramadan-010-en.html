Talbinah Newsletter – Special Ramadan Edition (1)
Ramadan is not a month of higher productivity…
It’s a month of a different rhythm
Hello,

Every year at the beginning of Ramadan, one sentence keeps repeating in workplaces:
“We reduce working hours… but increase productivity.”

But let’s be honest.
Ramadan is not a month of higher productivity.
It’s a month of a different rhythm.
And the difference between the two is significant.
🌙
Physiology Doesn’t Lie
During Ramadan, several things change:

Sleep patterns • Eating schedule • Blood sugar levels • Daily energy • Mental clarity

This isn’t a lack of willpower. It’s biology.
Numerous studies indicate that lack of sleep affects:

Decision-making speed • Emotional regulation • Ability to sustain focus

And during Ramadan specifically, many people:

Sleep less • Experience fragmented sleep • Go to bed later

So it’s completely natural for the rhythm to shift.
The question isn’t: “Why is the team less active?”
The better question is: “Did we design the day to fit Ramadan?”
💡
The Bottom Line
The common mistake during Ramadan is reducing working hours while keeping the same workload, the same number of meetings, and the same speed of expectations.
That’s when silent tension begins.

Ramadan requires:

Reduced intensity • Clear priorities • Respect for the body’s condition

Not motivational speeches.
🧠
