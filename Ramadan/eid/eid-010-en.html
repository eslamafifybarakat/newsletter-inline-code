Returning After the Holiday: Don't Rush… Start with Clarity
Hello,

The first day after the holiday is often busy.
Emails have piled up.
Meetings were postponed.
Goals feel like they "need attention immediately."

Question: Is the best way to return… speed? Or clarity?
🟢
The Reality After Long Holidays
After long holidays:

Temporary drop in focus
Increased anxiety due to backlog
Feeling pressure to prove a "serious return"

This is completely normal. The problem starts when we try to catch up with everything too fast.
💡
Key Takeaways
Rushing after the holiday does not mean progress.
Sometimes it is a psychological reaction to feeling guilty about "rest."

Mindful leadership after a holiday focuses on:
Reorganizing • Clarifying priorities • Setting the right pace
🧠
Smart Steps to Return After the Holiday
1- Only one meeting: "Organize, don't execute"
Short in duration, clear agenda:
What was completed before the holiday? • What is pending? • What should be paused?

2- Choose only 3 priorities for the week
Not 10, not everything.

3- Don't use quick responses as a performance measure
Calmness is more important than speed in the first week after the holiday.
A calm return is not weakness, but the shortest path to real progress
