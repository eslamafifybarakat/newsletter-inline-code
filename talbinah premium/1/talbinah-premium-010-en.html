We’re back… but not at the same pace
Hello,

The first week after a long holiday always feels different.
Work is back. Emails are back. Meetings are back.
But focus? Not necessarily at the same speed.
And that’s completely normal.
Harvard Business Review has explored the anxiety and pressure that come with returning to work after time off, recommending that we avoid trying to make a “full return” on the very first day — because adjustment requires space, not momentum. HBR also notes that post-vacation re-entry often brings an overwhelming pile of emails, unspoken expectations, and hidden stress. A smart restart happens gradually, not by overcompensating.
The problem isn’t returning itself.
The real issue is when we try to catch up on everything at once.

After extended breaks, most teams fall into one of these traps:
• More meetings than necessary
• Raising expectations too fast
• Mistaking speed for seriousness

But a mindful team understands that the first week needs:
Clarity, not urgency.
Structure, not chaos.
Prioritization, not an endless to-do list.
If you're leading a team this week, try this simple reset:

First: Don’t start with big initiatives.
Begin with one question: What are the 3 most important things we actually need to move forward this week?

Second: Make the first meeting about alignment, not execution.
A short meeting that answers:
What do we continue? What do we pause? What do we postpone?

Third: Lower expectations temporarily.
80% done with calm is far better than 120% with stress.
Returning after a holiday isn’t a test of how serious you are.
It’s a transition.
And a team that understands transition finds its rhythm faster… without burning out from the very beginning.
